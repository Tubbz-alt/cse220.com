Sparky

Introduction to Sparky

Sparky is a UNIX operating system, specifically SunOS version 5.10.

Sparky will be how you submit your assignments this semester.

Accessing Sparky

Sparky is hosted on the school's servers. You will be given credentials to login to Sparky over the SSH (Secure SHell) protocol.

Sparky can accessed via two different URLs:

  • sparky.ic.stonybrook.edu
  • sparky.ic.sunysb.edu

To sign into Sparky, open your command line program and enter the following:

sean@laptop [~] $ ssh -p 24 NETID@sparky.ic.stonybrook.edu

You will be prompted to enter your password. No text will appear while you type it. After you enter your password you must hit enter once more to get past a welcome screen. Once in your prompt will look like the following:

sparky%

Using the Shell

Sparky uses csh (C SHell) by default. We can confirm this by printing out the SHELL environment variable. To confirm, we echo it to standard out:

sparky% echo $SHELL
/bin/csh
sparky%

Sparky also has bash (Bourne Again SHell) available. As bash is the default shell on macOS and many versions of Linux, it is the recommended shell to use. It also supports more actions than csh which will improve your experience on Sparky. To launch bash from csh, type bash at the command line.

sparky% bash
bash-3.2$

Submitting Homework on Sparky

To submit on Sparky, run the following program: 

sparky% ~cse220/submit

Follow the ensuing prompts. The line count for your homework submission may vary by one line as a timestamp is appended to your homework file. Do not be concerned unless the line count reported varies by more than one line.
